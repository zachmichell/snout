# 6.6 — Processor fees, tips, and deferred revenue

Status: planned, not built. Each of the three flows below requires an
accounting policy decision the operator must make before any code lands.

## 6.6a — Processor fees (Stripe / Helcim)

When a customer pays $100 via Stripe, the operator's bank receives ~$97.
The $3 fee is netted out by the processor before deposit. Snout's
`payments.amount_cents` is the gross ($100); the operator's QBO needs
to see both:

- $100 income on the invoice (Payment as we already sync today)
- $3 expense on a "Merchant Processing Fees" account
- $97 net deposit to the operator's bank

The standard QBO pattern:

1. Create the Payment for $100 against Undeposited Funds.
2. When Stripe deposits to the bank, create a **Bank Deposit** in QBO
   that lists the $100 Payment as a deposit line and a negative $3
   "Merchant Processing Fees" line. Net deposit = $97, matching the
   bank statement.

This requires Snout to know:
- The processor's deposit batches (Stripe payouts API surfaces these
  as `Payout` objects; Helcim has a similar concept).
- A persistent mapping from Snout payment IDs to deposit batches.
- The fee per payment (Stripe returns this in `balance_transaction.fee`).

### Operator decisions needed

1. **Granularity.** Per-payment fees on every Bank Deposit (more
   detail, more lines), or one summary "Merchant Processing Fees"
   line per deposit batch (cleaner)? Recommendation: per-payment for
   audit, summary for daily ops. Default to summary and let operators
   toggle.
2. **Account names.** What QBO expense account holds processor fees?
   Sometimes "Merchant Processing Fees", sometimes "Bank Service
   Charges". Operator picks at QBO connect, persisted on
   `quickbooks_accounts`.
3. **Helcim parity.** Stripe and Helcim both expose payout APIs but
   the field shapes differ. Build Stripe first, Helcim second; share
   the deposit-batching code path.

### Schema additions

- `processor_payouts` table: id, organization_id, processor (stripe |
  helcim), processor_id, gross_cents, fee_cents, net_cents,
  payout_date, qbo_deposit_id (FK via mappings), state.
- Junction `payment_payouts` linking Snout payments to a payout.

### Edge function

`quickbooks-sync-payouts` runs nightly via cron, pulls payouts from
each connected processor for the org, builds Bank Deposit objects in
QBO with the gross + fee lines.

Estimated effort: 2-3 days plus operator review of first results.

## 6.6b — Tips

Currently invoice_lines has no first-class tip concept. Tips arrive
two ways:

1. As a separate line item on the invoice (description-only or
   service-mapped).
2. Added at payment time, after the invoice is finalized (Stripe
   "Tip Suggestion" pattern).

For the first path, no QBO work needed beyond what 6.4.5d already
ships — the line goes through with whatever tax code it's attached to.

For the second path, Snout would need a `tip_cents` column on
`payments` (or a separate `payment_tips` table), and the QBO Payment
sync would either:

- (a) Push a separate description-only line on the originating invoice
  retroactively. Awkward; the invoice was already synced.
- (b) Create a separate tiny invoice for the tip amount linked to the
  same customer, then a second Payment for it. Cleanest.
- (c) Sync the Payment with TotalAmt = invoice.total + tip and rely on
  the LinkedTxn applying invoice.total to the invoice and the leftover
  tip becoming an unapplied credit. Wrong; matches the bug we just fixed
  in 6.4.5d.

Recommendation: (b). Behind a feature flag until POS-style tipping is
actually used.

### Operator decisions needed

1. **Are tips happening today?** If grooming and daycare are mostly
   pre-priced and the operator doesn't take post-service tips, this
   whole flow can stay deferred indefinitely.
2. **Tip income account.** Many operators want tips on a separate
   liability/clearing account ("Employee Tips Payable") so they can
   distribute to staff later. Others book tips as service revenue.
   Operator confirms.

Estimated effort: 1-2 days when needed.

## 6.6c — Deferred revenue (prepayments)

When a customer prepays for a future booking — package credits,
boarding deposits, gift cards — accounting requires recording this
as a liability (Deferred Revenue), not as immediate income. Income
is recognized only when the service is delivered.

Snout has `daycare_full_day_credits`, `daycare_half_day_credits`,
`boarding_night_credits`, and `store_credit_cents` on the owners table.
These all accumulate from prepayments and are drawn down on bookings.

QBO modeling:

1. Prepayment received: Create a Payment that hits Undeposited Funds,
   plus a Journal Entry that debits Undeposited Funds and credits a
   "Deferred Revenue - Daycare" liability account. Net effect: cash up,
   liability up.
2. Service delivered: Journal Entry that debits the liability and
   credits the income account. Net effect: liability down, revenue
   recognized.

This is by far the most accounting-policy-heavy slice. It requires:

- Naming the liability account in QBO (one per credit type? one
  catch-all?).
- Defining when revenue is recognized — moment-of-booking, moment-of-
  service-completion, time-pro-rated for boarding.
- Handling expirations / write-offs for unused credits.

### Operator decisions needed

1. **Liability account structure.** One "Deferred Revenue" account
   total, or one per credit type (Daycare, Boarding, Store Credit)?
2. **Recognition trigger.** When does daycare credit become revenue?
   Strong recommendation: at the moment the day's daycare attendance
   is recorded, not when credits were sold. Ditto boarding nights at
   night-end.
3. **Expiration policy.** Snout has no expiration today. If credits
   never expire they sit on the books forever. If operators want
   expiration, they need a journal entry that moves expired credits
   from liability to income (with a special "Expired Credits Income"
   account, taxable status TBD).

Estimated effort: 4-5 days plus an operator review meeting on policy.

## Recommended sequence

When the operator returns:

1. **6.6a (fees) first.** Highest value, clearest accounting, no
   policy decisions besides account name. Stripe first, Helcim second.
2. **6.6c (deferred revenue) second.** Requires real policy meeting
   but unlocks correct month-end financials.
3. **6.6b (tips) only if needed.** Skip until tipping is actively
   happening.

Each can ship independently behind a feature flag.
